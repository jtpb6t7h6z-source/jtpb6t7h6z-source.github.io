Why I Ski

Some people discover skiing on a school trip and never look back. That's me. At age ten, my dad strapped wooden planks to my boots and pushed me down a green run at Breckenridge. I was terrified for about thirty seconds — and then completely hooked.

Skiing is the intersection of physics, athleticism, and raw nature. There's no pause button, no undo. Every turn is a negotiation between gravity, momentum, snow texture, and muscle memory. When it goes perfectly, you feel like you're flying. Even when it doesn't, you get up, brush the powder off, and try again. That philosophy has carried over into everything I do.

Carving through untouched powder — the best feeling in the world.

My Top Ski Resorts

Over the years I've skied more than twenty resorts across the Rockies, the Wasatch, and a handful of peaks in Europe. Here are my five favorites — and the specific runs that keep me coming back. Each resort is ranked as an ordered list, with the best individual runs listed below in an unordered list.

  1. Arapahoe Basin, Colorado — The Legend. True high-alpine skiing above 13,000 ft.
    • Pallavicini — a classic, steep, moguled wall that defines A-Basin
    • The Beavers — wide open groomers perfect for early morning speed runs
    • Montezuma Bowl — best above-treeline views in Colorado
    • East Wall — experts only; the reward is a run you'll brag about for years
  2. Jackson Hole Mountain Resort, Wyoming — For when you want to feel small.
    • Corbett's Couloir — the most iconic expert run in North America
    • Hobacks — long, rolling powder fields after a storm
    • Alta Chutes — technical, narrow, and absolutely exhilarating
  3. Snowbird, Utah — Greatest snow on Earth is not just a slogan.
    • Regulator Johnson — relentless sustained steep, every turn is commitment
    • Great Scott — wide open bowl skiing at its finest
    • Chip's Run — fast groomer to reset after a tough descent
  4. Telluride, Colorado — The most beautiful resort in the country.
    • Plunge — steep and direct, right under the lift so everyone watches
    • See Forever — the run matches the name; views to New Mexico on clear days
    • Gold Hill Glades — tree skiing with perfect spacing
  5. Whistler Blackcomb, British Columbia — An international benchmark.
    • Blackcomb Glacier — summer skiing and winter dreams combined
    • Spanky's Ladder — hike-to terrain that rewards the effort
    • Dave Murray Downhill — the Olympic DH course; go fast, tuck low

What's in My Quiver

Gear matters. Not because expensive equipment makes you a better skier, but because the right setup for the conditions can mean the difference between a transcendent powder day and a frustrating battle. Here's what I'm currently riding:

A well-tuned quiver is a skier's best friend.

All-Mountain Skis

Atomic Bent 100 (180 cm) — versatile, responsive, handles both groomers and powder days.

Powder Skis

Line Sick Day 114 (186 cm) — brought out on the big days. Floats effortlessly.

Boots

Tecnica Mach1 LV 130 — stiff, precise, custom footbed. Worth every penny.

Helmet & Goggles

Smith Vantage MIPS helmet + Smith I/O Mag goggles with chromapop lens. Safety first, style second.

Layers

Patagonia Nano-Air base, Arc'teryx Proton mid, Helly Hansen shell. The layering system is the system.

Backcountry Kit

Mammut avalanche beacon, Black Diamond probe and shovel. Never leave the trailhead without it.
